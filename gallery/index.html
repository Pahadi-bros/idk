Kumaon

Kumaon is a significant administrative division of the Indian state of Uttarakhand. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, breathtaking landscapes, and vibrant traditions. The region is surrounded by Tibet to the north, Nepal to the east, Uttar Pradesh to the south, and Garhwal to the west. It consists of six districts: Almora, Bageshwar, Champawat, Nainital, Pithoragarh, and Udham Singh Nagar. The Kumaoni people have a unique identity, with their own language, folk music, and traditions.

Haldwani

Haldwani, the "Gateway to Kumaon," is one of the largest cities in Uttarakhand. It serves as a commercial and educational hub, with easy access to the hill stations of the region. The city is known for its scenic beauty and pleasant climate.

Garhwal

Garhwal is a picturesque region nestled in the Himalayas, forming a vital part of Uttarakhand, India. Known for its rich history, spiritual significance, and breathtaking landscapes, Garhwal is home to sacred rivers, lush valleys, and snow-capped peaks. The region comprises districts such as Chamoli, Dehradun, Haridwar, Pauri Garhwal, Rudraprayag, Tehri Garhwal, and Uttarkashi. Garhwali people, known for their warm hospitality, speak the Garhwali language. With numerous temples, trekking trails, and serene hill stations, Garhwal is a haven for pilgrims, adventure seekers, and nature lovers alike.

Nanda Devi
